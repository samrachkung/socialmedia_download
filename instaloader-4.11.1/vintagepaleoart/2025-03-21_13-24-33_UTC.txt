Antrodemus (Allosaurus) and Stegosaurus by Bernard H. Robinson from the book A Ladybird Leader - Dinosaurs by Colin Douglas (1974). 

“Stegosaurus moved slowly. It had huge spikes on its tail. These ripped into attackers. Antrodemus hunted other animals.
Its mouth opened so wide it could swallow small animals whole.“

Note: Antrodemus valens is nowadays considered a nomen dubium (a scientific name that is of unknown or doubtful application). Its remains probably belonged to Allosaurus fragilis.
